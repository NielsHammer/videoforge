Most people think you need millions to live off dividends. Here's the real math.

Say you need four thousand dollars a month. That's forty-eight thousand a year. The average S&P 500 dividend yield is one point three percent. You'd need three point seven million dollars. Sounds impossible.

But smart investors don't buy the index for dividends. They buy high-yield stocks. Companies like Realty Income and Verizon pay five to seven percent. At six percent yield, you only need eight hundred thousand. That's a massive difference.

Here's what most people get wrong. They chase the highest yield without checking sustainability. A ten percent yield means nothing if the stock crashes forty percent. Always check the payout ratio. Above eighty percent is a red flag.

The real strategy is simple. Start now. Reinvest every dividend. Five hundred dollars a month for twenty years becomes over two hundred sixty thousand. That's real passive income. Start today.
