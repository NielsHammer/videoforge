Number one. The compounding dividend snowball.

Here's what most people get wrong about dividends. They think it's just about collecting quarterly payments. But the real magic happens when you reinvest those dividends to buy more shares. Those new shares generate more dividends, which buy even more shares.

Johnson and Johnson has increased its dividend for sixty-one straight years. If you'd invested ten thousand dollars in nineteen ninety, you'd own shares worth over one hundred thousand dollars today. Number two. The three percent rule that changes everything.

Professional dividend investors follow a simple guideline: never chase yields above six percent, and focus on companies yielding between two and four percent. Why? Because sustainable dividends come from profitable, stable companies.

Microsoft yields only point seven percent, but they've increased their dividend by over one thousand percent in the last twenty years. Meanwhile, that high-yield stock promising eight percent? It'll probably cut its dividend within five years, destroying your investment.
